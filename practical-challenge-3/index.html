The Mediterranean Diet

Overview

The Mediterranean Diet is a dietary pattern inspired by the traditional eating habits of countries bordering the Mediterranean Sea, characterized by high consumption of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, and olive oil, with moderate intake of fish, poultry, and dairy, and low consumption of red meat and sweets.

Health Benefits

Furthermore, research suggests that adhering to the Mediterranean Diet not only promotes physical health but also offers psychological benefits, such as reduced risk of depression and cognitive decline. Its emphasis on whole, minimally processed foods rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants provides a solid foundation for long-term health and vitality, making it a popular choice for those seeking a balanced and sustainable approach to nutrition.
