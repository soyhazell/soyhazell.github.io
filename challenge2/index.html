The Design of the 1960s

Overview

The 1960s was a decade of tremendous change, especially in the field of design. From the sleek lines of mid-century modernism to the bold colors and patterns of the psychedelic era, it was a time of innovation and experimentation.

Mid-Century Modernism

One of the defining styles of the 1960s was mid-century modernism. Characterized by clean lines, organic forms, and minimal ornamentation, mid-century modern design remains popular to this day.

Psychedelic Design

As the 1960s progressed, design became more experimental and playful. The psychedelic era brought with it bold colors, swirling patterns, and a sense of whimsy that reflected the spirit of the times.
